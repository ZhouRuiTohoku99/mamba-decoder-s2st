Enhancing Speech-to-Speech Translation with Mamba Decoder

In recent years, the MAMBA architecture has been increasingly adopted as an alternative to the Transformer model across various tasks, including speech recognition and speech synthesis, demonstrating superior performance in several cases. However, its efficacy in the domain of speech-to-speech translation (S2ST) has not been directly compared to that of the Transformer. In this study, we conduct a comprehensive evaluation of the MAMBA-decoder and Transformer-decoder within the context of S2ST. We assess their performance and efficiency using the CVSS-C corpus for Spanish-to-English and French-to-English translations. Our results indicate that the MAMBA architecture outperforms the Transformer, showing an improvement of 0.5 BLEU points for Spanish and 1.2 BLEU points for French. Moreover, MAMBA achieves comparable results to the Transformer in terms of inference quality while offering about a 31% decrease in inference time. These results demonstrate that MAMBA can match the Transformer in terms of inference capability while significantly enhancing inference efficiency.
